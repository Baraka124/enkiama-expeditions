Kilimanjaro

The mountain is not a
race to the top. We climb it
the way it deserves.

Africa’s highest point rewards patience, not speed. We compose a climb around acclimatisation, honesty, and a crew who are paid and treated as they should be — because how you reach the summit matters as much as whether you do.

Where we stand

Fewer people. More days.
Done properly.

There is an industry around this mountain that measures itself in numbers — how many climbers, how fast, how cheaply. We understand the appeal. We simply do not build our climbs that way.

A climb rushed to save a day or two is a climb more likely to fail, and more likely to make you ill. The body needs time to meet the altitude. So we add the days that give you the best true chance of standing on the summit — and the best chance of enjoying the climb that gets you there. More days is not us selling you more. It is the honest way up.

We would rather put fewer people on the summit, well, than the most people on the summit, fast.

The people who carry the mountain

A climb is only as honest as
the way it treats its crew.

No one summits Kilimanjaro alone. Behind every climber is a team — guides, cooks, and porters carrying what makes the mountain survivable. It is one of the hardest jobs in Tanzania, and across much of this industry it is quietly one of the worst paid, with loads too heavy, wages too low, and gear too thin for the cold up high.

We will not build a climb on that. Every porter and guide on an Enkiama climb is paid a fair wage, carries a fair load, and is properly equipped for the altitude — and we tell you who they are, by name, as we do with every partner in every journey. The people who carry your climb are not invisible labour. They are the reason you make it.

If an operator cannot tell you honestly how their crew is paid and treated, that silence is your answer. We would rather you ask us the hard question — and we will answer it plainly.

What we hold ourselves to

  • A wage set a quarter above the minimum advised by the recognised bodies — for every porter, cook, and guide, paid promptly after the climb, never withheld.
  • A load no porter should exceed — we keep to the twenty-kilogramme limit, and carry enough crew that no one is overburdened.
  • Three proper meals a day on the mountain, and gear warm enough for the altitude — not an afterthought, but part of the plan.
  • Every member of the crew named to you, and their tips paid to them in full.

These are the terms set by the Kilimanjaro Porters Assistance Project — the independent body that monitors how climbing crews are treated. We invite you to hold us, and any operator you consider, to them.

The route that fits you

The shortest route is not
the same as the right one.

There is a reason some operators steer everyone toward the quickest, cheapest path up the mountain: it moves more people, faster. But the shortest routes often carry the lowest success rates and the least time to acclimatise.

We choose the route around you — your fitness, your time, your experience, the season — not around our margin. Sometimes that means a longer, gentler line with better odds and finer scenery. Sometimes it means telling you honestly that now is not the moment, or that a different mountain, or the slopes rather than the summit, would serve you better.

The common way

The shortest line, for everyone

Fewer days, lower cost, more departures — and, quietly, lower success and less time to adjust to the height.

The Enkiama way

The right line, for you

The route matched to your body and your season, with the days acclimatisation actually needs. Composed for the summit — and for the climb itself.

Two ways to be changed by it

We will get you to the top,
if the top is your dream.

And we will tell you, just as honestly, that the mountain gives as much to those who never intend to summit it. Its southern slopes hold the Chagga farms and coffee that have shaped this region for centuries. Its forests, its moorland, its silence rearrange your sense of scale whether or not you reach the ice.

So we offer both, with a view: the summit for those who came for it, and the mountain for those who came to understand it. Neither is the lesser journey. Both are Kilimanjaro.

On every Enkiama climb

What holds, always.

  • The days acclimatisation needs — we build the climb around your safest true chance of the summit, not around a lower price.
  • A fairly paid, fairly loaded, properly equipped crew — named to you, as every partner is.
  • The route chosen for you — your fitness, season, and time, never our margin.
  • Honest odds — we tell you plainly what to expect of the altitude, and when not to attempt it.
  • The mountain’s people, not only its peak — the Chagga slopes and coffee are part of the journey, if you wish them.
  • Small — we take few climbers at a time, so the care never thins.

Whether you dream of the summit or the slopes, the climb begins the same way — with an honest conversation.
