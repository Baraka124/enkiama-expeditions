Barua — a letter home

You’ve been to Tanzania. Before it settles into memory, tell us what you carried home.

Not a review. A letter. Write to us the way you’d write to a friend who asked how it was — and if you allow it, we may one day share your words with someone standing where you once stood, wondering whether to go.

“I thought I was going to see animals. I came back having been somewhere quieter than I knew existed. The morning the migration crossed the river, no one in our vehicle said a word for an hour. I still haven’t, really.”
