Journeys composed  ·  Never repeated

What a journey
actually looks like.

Every Enkiama journey is built from scratch for one group and never offered again. These are the shapes of a few we have composed: the routes, the rhythms, the worlds they moved through. The detail of each lives in a private document we share only with the people travelling. If one of these resembles what is pulling at you, ask us to walk you through it in full.

Kilimanjaro → Serengeti → Rift Valley → Zanzibar
13 nights 4 worlds 9 lodges 3 guides

Five friends,
first time in Tanzania.

A group of five who wanted the whole arc: mountain culture, the migration on the open plain, the descent into the crater, and the slow exhale of the coast to finish. Composed around their pace and their appetite for both spectacle and stillness, with a cultural day in Mto wa Mbu in place of a fourth park. July, peak migration. Travelling as a group, or forming one →

Mountain · Savanna · Rift Valley · Coast  ·  July 2026

Tarangire → Serengeti → Ngorongoro
8 nights 3 worlds 2 guides

A wildlife journey,
nothing but the land.

A couple who came for one thing: animals, and the people who can read them. No coast, no cities. Tarangire’s elephants, the northern Serengeti at the height of the crossing season, and a full day in the crater. Slow mornings, long game drives, a guide who had spent thirty years on that plain.

Savanna · Crater  ·  August

Kilimanjaro foothills → Kibosho → Moshi
6 nights 2 worlds 1 family host

The mountain,
without the summit.

A journey for travellers who wanted the mountain’s culture rather than its peak: the Chagga coffee farms at 1,700 metres, a homestay with a family who have worked the same slopes for four generations, the cloud forest, and an evening in Moshi that no itinerary planned. Quiet, human, and completely unlike a safari. How we approach the mountain →

Mountain · Culture  ·  June

Serengeti → Stone Town → Pemba
11 nights 2 worlds 2 guides

Plain and ocean,
one long exhale.

A journey built on contrast: the intensity of the savanna followed by the slow rhythm of the coast. Days on the open plain, then a flight to Zanzibar: Stone Town’s seven centuries of Swahili history with a historian who knows every doorway, and the untouched reefs of Pemba to finish. The two halves spoke to each other.
