Before you begin  ·  The real questions

Questions,
answered honestly.

The things people actually want to know before a Tanzania safari: when to come, what it costs, whether it is safe, how the migration works. Straight answers, the way we would give them in conversation.

When is the best time to go on a Tanzania safari?

Tanzania is rewarding year-round. The dry season, June to October, offers the easiest game viewing and the famous Serengeti river crossings. The green season, December to March, brings the wildebeest calving, fewer crowds, and lush landscapes. There is no bad time, only different gifts.

How much does a private Tanzania safari cost?

A bespoke Tanzania journey is priced around the specific itinerary, season, lodges, and group. Rather than sell fixed packages, we compose each journey around your budget and shape the experience to fit it. The price we give is honest, complete, and final, with no hidden extras. We discuss it openly, in conversation.

Is Tanzania safe for travellers?

Tanzania is one of the more stable and welcoming countries in East Africa, and safari areas are well managed. Travelling with a trusted local team, experienced guides, and vetted lodges is the single biggest factor in a safe, smooth journey, which is exactly how we compose every trip.

What is the Great Migration, and when can I see it?

The Great Migration is the year-round movement of over a million wildebeest and zebra across the Serengeti and into Kenya. The dramatic Mara River crossings happen roughly July to October; the calving season is December to March in the southern Serengeti. The herds move constantly, so timing and location are matched to what you most want to see.

Can I go on safari with children?

Yes. Tanzania can be wonderful with children when the pace, lodges, and activities are chosen for the family. Shorter drives, family-friendly camps, and interactive experiences make a real difference, and a private journey lets everything flex around your family rather than a fixed group.

How many days do I need for a Tanzania safari?

Most rewarding journeys run seven to fourteen days. A week allows the classic northern circuit; ten days to two weeks lets you move through several distinct worlds, add the wilder south or west, or finish on the Zanzibar coast without rushing.

Do you arrange safaris for Spanish-speaking travellers?

Yes. We work fluently with Spanish-speaking travellers and can compose the entire journey, guiding, and communication in Spanish, so nothing is lost in translation before, during, or after the trip.

What is a curated safari, and how is it different from a package tour?

A package tour is sold from a shelf: fixed route, fixed dates, fixed group. A curated journey is composed around you, one group at a time, choosing each lodge, guide, and moment for a reason. You are never sold a product; the journey is written with you.
